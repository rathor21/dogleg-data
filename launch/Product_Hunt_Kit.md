## Listing

**Name:** Dogleg Data
**Tagline (48 chars, limit 60):** Golf's conventional wisdom, tested at your handicap
**Topics:** Sports, Data & Analytics, Free tools
**Links:** https://doglegdata.com (primary), dashboard as the product URL

**Description:**
> Free interactive golf decision models for the 8-to-30 handicap. Most golf advice was computed from tour data; Dogleg Data reruns the math at your handicap. A break-even explorer (how much is the fairway worth at your distance), a Monte Carlo GIR simulator, and a scramble decision calculator. Every source named on the chart, every modeled number labeled, peer-review notes published. No signup, no ads.

**Gallery (in order):** quote card (launch/quote_card_150yd.png), chart 1, chart 2, dashboard screenshot (take on production), chart 5. First image is the social preview; the quote card is built for that.

**Maker's first comment:**
> I'm a data analyst and a mid-handicap golfer, and I got tired of course advice computed from tour numbers. "Always play the fairway ball" is right for the pros; at a 20-handicap the math changes, and past 240 yards it flips entirely.
>
> So I built the thing I wanted: decision models rebuilt at amateur handicaps from published data (Broadie's ShotLink work, Lou Stagner's Arccos numbers, USGA/R&A rough studies). Everything modeled is labeled modeled, and I published what peer review caught before launch, because an error I catch is content and an error you catch is damage.
>
> It's free and there's no signup. What I'd love from this community: break the model. Bring a scramble read or a distance question and I'll run it through, and if the method has a hole, tell me where.
