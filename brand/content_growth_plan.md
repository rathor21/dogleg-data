# Dogleg Data — Content & Growth Plan

90 days, three phases, one person with a day job. Budget: 4-6 hours per week. Everything here assumes the launch package (Fairway vs. Rough LinkedIn post, X thread, interactive dashboard) is rebranded, peer-reviewed, and ready.

Date: 2026-07-01 · Status: draft for Sunny's review

---

## Operating constraints

- **Time:** one flagship analysis per month at most. Everything else reuses the model, the charts, or the audience.
- **Platforms:** X posts as the brand, first person singular. LinkedIn posts come from Sunny personally with the plug line "Full model at Dogleg Data." Every chart carries the badge and source line, because charts travel farther than profiles.
- **Quality bar:** nothing publishes without a source line, and modeled numbers stay labeled modeled. The peer-review habit is the brand's signature move; keep publishing what broke.

### The Scramble Reads format (recurring, audience-owned)

The launch post already ends with the invite. Turn it into a fixture:

1. Reader sends a real scramble situation by reply or DM: distances, lies, rough type, handicaps.
2. Run it through the scramble decision calculator. Produce one chart-2-style graphic: the read, the break-even number, the verdict.
3. Post it, credit the submitter by handle, restate the invite.

One per week, thirty minutes each, infinite supply once it catches. It also generates the inbound-DM metric below without any outreach.

### Engagement rule

Respond with charts, never with takes. When Lou Stagner, an analytics account, or a podcast raises a question the model can answer, answer it in a reply with a chart inside 48 hours, source line on the image. This is the whole earned-citation engine; opinions without a chart attached stay in drafts.

### How citations get earned (standing rules)

1. **Tag the source every time their data appears.** Stagner, Arccos, Broadie, GOLFTEC, USGA/R&A. Tags on the post, source lines on the chart. Goodwill compounds; so does its absence.
2. **Charts, never takes** (rule above). Podcasters cite artifacts, and a chart with a badge is an artifact.
3. **Pitch finished work only.** Every pitch contains a completed, credited chart the recipient can use as-is. The only ask: "if it's useful, it's yours." No calls, no "would you be interested," no follow-up sequence. One polite nudge after two weeks, then done.

### Warm-pitch template (Golfwell / Benoit)

> Hi [name]. I run Dogleg Data, an independent golf analytics project: real data on what the numbers say for a normal golfer, sources cited, modeled numbers labeled.
>
> On [episode / video], you asked [specific question]. I had built most of the answer already, so I finished it: [one-sentence finding, with the source named]. The chart is attached, sized for [show notes / on-screen], credit line already on the image. If it's useful, it's yours, no ask beyond that.
>
> The interactive model behind it is here if you want to test your own numbers: [dashboard link].

Adjust one line per recipient. For Benoit, name the specific piece of his that prompted the chart.

## Path to brand collabs

### What a brand needs to see before saying yes

- **A consistent visual identity.** Badge on every chart, cream paper, fixed tier colors. A brand manager should recognize a Dogleg chart with the wordmark cropped out.
- **Cadence they can set a calendar by.** Twelve-plus weeks without ghosting. A skipped week gets announced, never silently dropped.
- **Engagement quality over volume.** Saves and reposts from credible analytics and coaching accounts, submitted scramble reads, a citation or two. A small audience that includes the right hundred people beats a large one that includes nobody they know.

### Milestones before any brand pitch

- 6+ published analyses or substantial posts
- 12+ consecutive weeks of cadence
- 1+ podcast or YouTube citation (this is what phase 3 exists to produce)
- Scramble Reads running with real audience submissions
- Trademark search on "Dogleg Data" completed clean (see risks)

### Which brands fit

| Brand | Fit | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Arccos | Strongest data fit; their dataset already underpins the analyses | Employs Lou Stagner. That relationship is the warmest path in and a conflict to manage: a paid Arccos deal colors every future analysis built on their data. Any collab needs disclosure and a written understanding that findings publish as found. |
| Shot Scope | Publishes its own amateur performance data; a natural second dataset | An analysis built on their published data, credited, is itself the pitch. |
| Rapsodo / launch-monitor category (Garmin Approach, FlightScope Mevo class) | Dispersion and gapping data matches the model's inputs | Fits the "bring your own numbers through the dashboard" angle. |

Independence is the asset a brand is buying, and it evaporates the moment a reader suspects the numbers bend toward a sponsor. Every collab conversation starts from that sentence.

## Measurement

Five numbers, reviewed on the last Sunday of each month, twenty minutes, logged in one sheet. Follower count gets recorded and ignored.

| Metric | Why it matters | Where to find it |
|---|---|---|
| Saves + reposts by analytics/coaching accounts | Distribution among the people podcasters listen to | X analytics, repost notifications |
| Podcast / YouTube mentions | The stated goal of the whole plan | Manual: episode notes, mention searches |
| Inbound DMs (scramble reads, data questions, collab feelers) | Demand arriving without outreach | DM inbox count, by type |
| Scramble reads submitted per month | Health of the recurring format | Replies + DMs |
| Dashboard sessions per post | Whether readers engage the model or scroll past the chart | Link clicks (add a simple counter or short-link stats) |

## Risks and watch-outs

| Risk | Watch signal | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Single-analysis launch | Launch spike, then silence | Pipeline before promotion: weeks 2-5 drafted before launch day. Outreach waits until four posts are live. |
| Data-source goodwill | Any complaint or cooling from Stagner/Arccos | Never scrape or repost their tables without credit. Tag on post, source line on chart, link the original newsletter, quote excerpts rather than reproducing full tables. When in doubt, ask first. |
| Dogleg name collision | Adjacent "Dogleg" marks exist (Swiss apparel, a brewery, a putter maker); confusion or a demand letter | None are analytics content and "Dogleg Data" is unclaimed, per the brand spec. Monitor mentions monthly. Run a real trademark search before spending money on the name (merch, paid promotion, incorporation). |
| Burnout against the day job | Missed weeks, quality slippage | Cadence is set at the sustainable floor, one flagship per month. Announce skip weeks. A slower drumbeat beats a broken one. |
| Modeled-number credibility | A reader catches an unlabeled extrapolation | Labeling discipline from the brand spec, plus the standing habit of publishing what peer review catches. Errors admitted early are content; errors found by others are damage. |
