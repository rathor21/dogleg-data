# Impeccable Design Config — Dogleg Data

## Design Context

### Users
The 8-to-30 handicap golfer who plays $40 munis and gets hand-me-down advice built from tour numbers. Secondary: the LinkedIn data community, golf podcasters and YouTubers (Golfwell, Dr. Luke Benoit) who need citable artifacts, and golf brands evaluating a collab. They arrive from an X thread or LinkedIn post, usually on a phone, wanting to read the analysis and poke at the interactive model. The job to be done: "tell me what the numbers say for a golfer like me, and show your work."

### Brand Personality
Three words: rigorous, warm, unhyped. The interface should evoke earned trust: every claim sourced, every modeled number labeled. Voice rules: second person over abstraction, active voice, no em dashes, no filler adverbs, no "not X, it's Y" reversals, no hype vocabulary ("game-changer," "unlock"). Talks to the 15-handicap, quotes the tour data.

### Aesthetic Direction
Malbon-inspired: vintage country club meets streetwear. Retro-leisure over lab-report. Badge and mascot energy, flat screen-print surfaces, cream paper, leather-goods tones. Data credibility lives in the details: mono numbers, source lines, modeled-data flags. Anti-references: white-background SaaS dashboards, dark "sports betting" UIs, anything neon, anything with green or yellow (banned brand-wide).

Reference implementation: `Golf Agent/Fairway_vs_Rough_Post/Fairway_vs_Rough_Dashboard.html` is the canonical, already-approved design system in code. Match it.

Fixed handicap tier mapping everywhere: tour = ink, 10 = denim, 20 = clay, 30 = oxblood (dashed + starred as modeled). Color is never the only signal.

### Design Principles
1. **Cream paper, never white.** Pages sit on #F4EDE0. Charts sit inside a lighter cream card (#FBF7EE) so they separate from the page instead of blending into it. No green, no yellow, anywhere.
2. **Show the work in the chrome.** Source lines in mono under every chart, "modeled" badges (dashed oxblood pill) wherever a modeled number appears.
3. **Badge energy, restrained.** Double-rule borders (3px double var(--line)), circular badge motifs, the dogleg mark, mono uppercase micro-labels with wide tracking. The mascot dachshund appears in small doses: footer, empty states, 404.
4. **Type does the branding.** Fraunces 900 for the wordmark and display headlines, Fraunces 600 for section heads, Plex Mono for anything numeric, Inter for reading. Wordmark is always live text, never an image.
5. **Fast and self-contained.** Static HTML/CSS, no build step, no frameworks. Chart.js via CDN only where the dashboard needs it. Accessible: WCAG AA contrast on cream, :focus-visible styles, prefers-reduced-motion respected, alt text that states the chart's finding.
