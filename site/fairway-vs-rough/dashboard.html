Simulated chance of hitting the green

Monte Carlo shot-dispersion model, distance-aware via real Stagner/Arccos proximity data. Treat the output as an illustration of the model rather than an official GIR benchmark.
This models single-shot proximity, so it misses the long-range rough roll-out effect that shows up in the real Stagner scoring data (see the post). Tour dispersion assumes pros are 25% tighter than a scratch amateur; that figure is a judgment call rather than a cited number.

Sources & methodology

Tour numbers: Broadie (2011), ShotLink Table 9, 8M+ shots. Amateur break-even: Lou Stagner / Arccos, ~1B shots (10/20-index published; 25/30-index extrapolated across the full published 0-20 range). Amateur proximity-by-distance: Stagner/Arccos newsletter #71, used here to make the GIR tool's distance sensitivity real rather than flat. Rough-severity scaling: 2021 USGA/R&A rough-difficulty simulation, cross-checked against a 2025 Golf Digest field test. Dispersion simulation calibrated to GOLFTEC 7-iron fitting data, extrapolated beyond its measured range for higher handicaps and stretched to the full distance range using real proximity-by-distance data. That simulation is the most model-dependent piece of this tool.
